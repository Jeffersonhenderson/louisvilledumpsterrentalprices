Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the main difference between a roll-off dumpster and a standard commercial dumpster?

A roll off dumpster is an open-top container designed for temporary projects with large volumes of waste, like construction or cleanouts. They are "rolled off" a specialized truck into place. A standard commercial dumpster is the smaller, lidded container you typically see behind businesses, which is emptied by a front-loading truck on a regular, permanent schedule.

2. Can I request a specific time for my dumpster delivery?

While we cannot guarantee an exact delivery time due to the dynamic nature of traffic and routing, we do offer delivery windows (e.g., morning or afternoon). We will always do our best to accommodate your schedule and will communicate with you on your delivery day.

3. What should I do to prepare my property for the dumpster's arrival?

Please ensure that the area where you want the roll off bin in Boron, CA placed is clear of any obstacles, such as vehicles, equipment, or debris. Our trucks require a significant amount of clear, straight space to back in, so clearing a path of at least 50-60 feet is ideal.

4. Is it possible to put heavy materials like concrete or dirt in any dumpster?

Due to Department of Transportation weight regulations, very heavy materials like concrete, asphalt, brick, or dirt must be placed in a dedicated small roll off dumpster (usually a 10 yd roll off) and cannot be mixed with other types of debris.

5. How is the final cost of my dumpster rental calculated?

Your roll off dumpster rental cost in Boron, CA is typically based on three main factors: the size of the container you rent, the length of the rental period, and the final weight of the debris you dispose of. Our quotes include a generous weight allowance, and we are transparent about any potential costs.

6. What happens if the dumpster damages my driveway?

Newtown Dumpster Rentals is not responsible for damage to property surfaces like driveways or lawns. The combined weight of the truck and the roll off dumpster is significant. We highly recommend that customers place sheets of plywood on the surface where the container will be placed to help distribute the weight and protect the surface.

Boron, CA Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Boron (formerly Amargo, Baker, Borate, and Kern) is a census-designated place (CDP) in Kern County, California, United States. Boron is 15 miles (24 km) southwest of Red Rock Mountain at an elevation of 2,467 feet (752 m). The population was 2,086 at the 2020 census, up from 2,025 at the 2000 census. Boron is named after the element boron and is the site of the world's largest source of the boron compound boric acid.
